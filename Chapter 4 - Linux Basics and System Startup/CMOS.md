- Small, battery (called CMOS Battery - usually CR2032) - powered chipset keeping track of time and hardware configuration, BIOS passwords
- 1 battery should last about 5-10 years
- If you need to **reset it**, simply remove the battery for a 10-15 seconds
- Some motherboard also provide a way to reset the battery to reset CMOS memory in a way of a button to press or jumper 
-  most motherboards have a clearly labelled `CLR_CMOS` or `JBAT1` jumper near the battery.

### Signs of dead CMOS battery

- Clock resets to 1970 (Unix epoch)  or manufacturer date on every boot
- [[BIOS]] settings don't persist
- "CMOS checksum error" on boot
